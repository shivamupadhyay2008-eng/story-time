The Midnight Vow
Chapter Twenty

The Silent Corridor

In the architecture of this school, walls have ears, and floors have pressure sensors. Every step is a gamble, and every shadow is a potential grave. But we weren't just walking through a building anymore; we were walking through a system that we had finally learned how to break.

The administration wing was a stark departure from the frantic, blood-red carnage of the Atrium. It was silent, sterile, and lit by a cold, unwavering white light. We reached the heavy reinforced door of the Server Hub—the brain of the school.

"I need five minutes," Lily whispered. She slid the access card into the scanner. The door hissed open.

Inside, Lily’s fingers flew across the interface. "I’m in. I’m pulling the student manifests... Bruce, there’s something else here. A sub-directory labeled 'The Contingency.' It’s a list of students who were never supposed to be heirs. They were brought here to be the 'variables'—to take out the High Council from the inside."

I turned, my jaw tightening. "Variables? Like us?"

"Like you," she corrected. "They knew who your father was. They haven't been trying to break you; they've been testing to see if you were strong enough to survive this."

The corridor suddenly erupted with the agonizing frequency of an ultrasonic dampener. Three Wardens—private security—stepped out of the shadows. Lily dragged me into the server room, slamming the steel door shut.

We were trapped in the dark, the Wardens cutting through the hinges. I pushed her gently against the vibrating metal of the server bank, my thumb grazing her lower lip. "They want a variable? Then let's show them what happens when the math goes wrong."

I pulled the primary power relay. The wing plunged into suffocating darkness.

"Bruce," Lily whispered in the dark, her hand finding mine with a desperate, crushing grip. "If we die in this room, there is a file... the Architect's journal. You need to see it."

The heavy door hit the floor with a deafening crash. The Wardens were inside.

I pulled the pistol from her hand. "Save it for the after-party," I breathed, stepping into the blackness as the room exploded in white-hot muzzle flashes.

As I fired, my hand brushed the terminal. The screen hummed to life, displaying a single, glowing folder: //DECRYPTED_ARCHITECT_LOGS
