The Midnight Vow
Chapter Twenty

The Silent Corridor

In the architecture of this school, walls have ears, and floors have pressure sensors. Every step is a gamble, and every shadow is a potential grave. But we weren't just walking through a building anymore; we were walking through a system that we had finally learned how to break.

The administration wing was a stark departure from the frantic, blood-red carnage of the Atrium. It was silent, sterile, and lit by a cold, unwavering white light. We reached the heavy reinforced door of the Server Hub—the brain of the school.

"I need five minutes," Lily whispered. She slid the access card into the scanner. The door hissed open.

Inside, Lily’s fingers flew across the interface. "I’m in. I’m pulling the student manifests... Bruce, there’s something else here. A sub-directory labeled 'The Contingency.' It’s a list of students who were never supposed to be heirs. They were brought here to be the 'variables'—to take out the High Council from the inside."

I turned, my jaw tightening. "Variables? Like us?"

"Like you," she corrected, her voice losing its clinical edge. She turned away from the glowing screen, stepping close enough that I could feel the faint, shaky exhale of her breath. "They knew who your father was. They haven't been trying to break you, Bruce. They've been testing to see if you were strong enough to survive this."

She reached up, her cold fingers gently tracing the line of my jaw. It wasn't the frantic, adrenaline-fueled grip of survival we usually shared. It was impossibly soft. A delicate tether pulling me back from the edge of the abyss. Her thumb brushed over my cheekbone, grounding me in the sterile room.

"You are more than what they designed you to be," she whispered, her dark eyes searching mine with a profound, quiet ache.

I leaned into her touch, my hand coming up to rest over hers. For a fractured second, the war didn't exist. There was only the quiet hum of the servers and the startling warmth of her palm against my skin. I traced the knuckles of her hand, entirely captivated by the girl who could hack a heavily encrypted mainframe and still look at me like I was the only thing that mattered.

Then, the corridor outside erupted.

The agonizing frequency of an ultrasonic dampener shattered the quiet. Three Wardens—private security—stepped out of the shadows. Lily immediately dropped her hand, grabbing my jacket and dragging me deeper into the server room as she slammed the steel door shut.

We were trapped in the dark, the Wardens cutting through the hinges. I pushed her gently against the vibrating metal of the server bank, shielding her with my body. "They want a variable? Then let's show them what happens when the math goes wrong."

I pulled the primary power relay. The wing plunged into suffocating darkness.

"Bruce," Lily whispered in the pitch black, her free hand finding the collar of my shirt, pulling me down just enough to press her forehead against mine. It was a fiercely tender gesture, a promise forged in the dark. "If we die in this room, there is a file... the Architect's journal. You need to see it."

The heavy door hit the floor with a deafening crash. The Wardens were inside.

I pulled the pistol from her hand. "Save it for the after-party," I breathed, brushing a soft kiss against her temple before stepping into the blackness as the room exploded in white-hot muzzle flashes.

As I fired, my hand brushed the terminal. The screen hummed to life, displaying a single, glowing folder: //DECRYPTED_ARCHITECT_LOGS
