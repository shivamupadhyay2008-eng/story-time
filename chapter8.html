The Midnight Vow
Chapter Eight

The Ledger

People think keeping a secret is about staying quiet. It isn't. Keeping a secret is a full-time job. It requires maintenance, paranoia, and most importantly... a paper trail.

Back in my dorm, with Jack still at football practice, I locked the door and tossed Julian’s leather-bound planner onto my desk.

At first glance, it looked exactly like what you would expect from the school's golden boy. Color-coded study schedules, debate team strategies, and college prep meetings. But as I flipped toward the back, the neat handwriting devolved into a frantic, jagged scrawl.

Pages of numbers. Dates. And a repeating set of initials: W.H.

William Harrison. The finance director.

I grabbed a notebook and started cross-referencing the dates. Julian wasn't just tracking meetings. This was a ledger. And it was heavily encrypted.

But encryption only works if the person writing it is smarter than the person reading it. Julian was smart. I am a different breed entirely.

It took me less than forty minutes to break his simple substitution cipher. As the numbers translated into dollar amounts, a beautiful, horrifying picture began to form. Julian was bleeding money. Thousands of dollars transferred in untraceable increments from the debate team's endowment, straight into an offshore account controlled by Harrison.

But why? Why would a rich kid with a flawless future risk federal prison to steal school funds?

I kept decoding. The answer was buried on the very last page, written in a moment of pure panic on the night of October 14th of last year.

10/14 - 2:00 AM. Route 9. The girl on the bike. W.H. saw the car. Paid 10k to wipe the traffic cams. It isn't enough.

I sat back in my chair, a slow, cold smile spreading across my face.

A hit-and-run. The untouchable Julian Vance got drunk, hit a local girl on the highway, and paid Harrison to cover his tracks. But a degenerate gambler like Harrison is never satisfied. He had been bleeding Julian dry ever since.

The Headmaster had told me to find a mistake so damning that Julian would belong to the Crescent forever. He wanted me to put Julian on a leash.

I am going to do so much more than that. I am going to wrap the leash around his neck until he chokes.

I ripped the decoded pages from my notebook, folded them neatly, and slipped them into a fresh white envelope. Then, I wrote a single line on the outside in immaculate handwriting:

I know about Route 9. Meet me in the old greenhouse at midnight. Come alone.

The trap is set. Now, we wait for the golden boy to walk willingly into the dark.
