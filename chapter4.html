The Midnight Vow
Chapter Four

The Midnight Encounter

My hands actually shook. For the first time in my life, the cold logic that kept me safe cracked.

Was any of it real? The car crash, the lonely years with my uncle, my arrival at this exact school? Or was I just a pawn being moved exactly where they wanted me, on a board I couldn’t even see?

I shoved the photograph deep into my jacket pocket.

Panic is a luxury I can't afford. Not tonight.

I closed my eyes, took a slow, calculated breath, and buried the fear under a thick layer of ice. The confused, scared kid was gone. The strategist was back.

I slipped out of the office, re-locking the heavy brass door behind me. The corridors were pitch black. I kept to the shadows, my footsteps completely silent against the stone floor. But just as I turned the final corner toward the dormitories, a soft voice shattered the silence.

"Bruce?"

I froze. My muscles tensed for a fraction of a second. Then, effortlessly, the mask slid perfectly back into place. I turned around, painting a gentle, mildly surprised smile on my face.

It was Lily. She was wrapped in an oversized cardigan, holding an empty glass.

"Lily," I whispered, keeping my tone warm and perfectly even. "What are you doing up this late?"

"I could ask you the same thing," she said softly, taking a step closer. The moonlight from the window caught her eyes.

She’s looking for a connection. She thinks we are just two innocent insomniacs sharing a quiet, romantic moment in the dark.

"I just couldn't sleep," I lied, my voice dripping with polite vulnerability. "I needed to clear my head. You know how the walls here can feel like they're closing in sometimes?"

She softened immediately, a sympathetic smile forming on her lips. "I know exactly what you mean. Goodnight, Bruce."

"Goodnight, Lily. Get some rest."

I walked away at a measured, casual pace, being careful not to look back. But the second I was behind my closed dorm room door, the charming smile completely vanished from my face.

Jack was still snoring loudly. I sat on the edge of my bed and pulled the photograph back out in the dark. My eyes locked onto the younger face of our Headmaster.

I need to know everything about him. I need to know his schedule, where he goes after hours, who he speaks to, and exactly what that symbol means. I am going to stalk the man who runs this prison. I will pull apart his life piece by piece, and if he had a hand in my parents' death... I won't just punish him. I'll destroy him.

But how exactly do you hunt a powerful man from inside his own cage? And when I finally make my move... will I actually survive it?
