The Midnight Vow
Chapter Twenty-Two

The Descent

Violence is a fever. Once it sets into your blood, it doesn't just change how you see the world; it changes how you feel it. Every breath burns, every shadow feels heavy, and the space between you and the person standing next to you becomes agonizingly thin.

The radio crackled one last time before I crushed it under the heel of my boot.

"The underground extraction point," Lily whispered, her eyes tracking the dark corridors of the server room. "The old Prohibition-era transit tunnels beneath the foundation. If the Headmaster reaches the private rail line, he vanishes. The Crescent wins."

"Then we don't let him reach the train," I said.

We slipped out of the Server Hub and into the service stairwell, descending into the absolute bowels of the school. The temperature plummeted with every floor. The polished marble gave way to cracked concrete, rusting iron pipes, and the damp, heavy scent of subterranean earth.

The emergency lights down here were failing, flickering in weak, dying pulses of amber.

We reached the sub-basement security gate—a heavy iron portcullis meant to seal off the tunnels. It was locked down, but the maintenance alcove beside it had a narrow, rusted-out ventilation breach.

"I can fit," Lily murmured, sliding her pistol into her waistband. "But you're going to have to follow close. If the structural iron shifts, it’s a dead end."

She climbed through the jagged opening first. I followed immediately after. The shaft was brutally narrow, forcing us to crawl through the absolute dark, surrounded by freezing, jagged metal. We were moving blind, relying entirely on touch.

Suddenly, the heavy echo of combat boots vibrated through the concrete above us. A sweep team.

Lily froze in the shaft, pressing herself flat against the metal. I was right behind her, my chest flush against her back. The space was so tight there was nowhere else to go.

"Don't move," she breathed, her voice barely a vibration in the dark.

I didn't. I was pressed against her, the heat of her body radiating through the torn fabric of her clothes, a stark, feverish contrast to the freezing iron pressing against my sides. The tactical sweep team passed directly over the ceiling grate above us, their flashlights slicing thin, blinding slivers of white light through the dust.

We stayed locked together in the suffocating dark. Every time she inhaled, her spine pressed firmly against my chest. The friction of our bodies, trapped in that tiny space, was maddening. The adrenaline of the slaughter upstairs hadn't faded; it had pooled into this heavy, undeniable gravity between us.

The boots faded down the corridor. The sweep team was gone.

We crawled the last ten feet and dropped out of the shaft into the primary transit tunnel. The air here was vast, echoing, and completely dark save for a single amber light at the far end of the tunnel.

Lily leaned back against the damp concrete wall, her chest heaving as she caught her breath. She looked at me in the dim, flickering light. Her dark hair was falling wildly around her shoulders, her skin glowing with a thin sheen of sweat, her eyes completely blown out with adrenaline.

I didn't step back. I stepped into her space, crowding her against the cold wall.

"We have ten minutes before the train departs," I said, my voice dropping to a low, rough rasp.

"I know," she whispered.

But neither of us moved toward the amber light.

Instead, Lily reached out, her hands sliding under the ruined fabric of my jacket. Her cold fingers traced the rigid, tense muscles of my stomach, sending a violent jolt of electricity straight to my chest. She didn't stop there. Her hands slid up, her palms pressing flat against my chest, feeling the frantic, hammering rhythm of the Architect’s blood in my veins.

"You're burning up," she murmured, her gaze dropping to my lips.

I grabbed her wrists, pinning her hands against my chest. "So are you."

I let go of her wrists and slid my hands down to her waist, my fingers gripping her hips with a bruising, possessive force. I pulled her flush against me, lifting her slightly so her back arched against the concrete. She gasped, a soft, sharp sound that echoed in the empty tunnel, her hands instantly flying up to tangle in my hair.

I buried my face in the curve of her neck, my lips tracing the hot, frantic pulse beating wildly against her skin. The smell of her—vanilla, ozone, and the metallic tang of blood—was intoxicating. She let out a ragged breath, her nails digging viciously into my shoulders as I bit down lightly on her collarbone, feeling her skin heat under my mouth.

Every point where we touched felt like a live wire. My hand slid down her thigh, gripping the bare, soft skin exposed by her torn skirt, pulling her leg up around my hip to anchor her against me. The friction of our bodies pressing together in the dark was agonizingly intense, fueled by the terrifying reality that we were about to walk into a firing squad.

She pulled my head up, her eyes dark, heavy, and completely feral.

"When this is over," she breathed, her lips brushing against mine with every word, "when we own this school... you are mine, Bruce. Completely."

"I was yours the second you stitched my hand," I whispered back, my grip on her thigh tightening.

I kissed her—a deep, brutal, breathless collision that tasted like a promise of absolute ruin. For a minute, the dark tunnel faded, leaving only the feverish heat of her mouth, the desperate grip of her hands, and the lethal certainty of what we belonged to.

RUMBLE.

A deep, mechanical vibration shook the concrete beneath our boots. The sound of massive diesel engines turning over echoed down the tunnel from the amber light.

The extraction train was powering up.

We broke apart, our breathing ragged, our eyes locked. The fever was still there, burning right beneath the surface, but the predator in both of us took control.

Lily let her leg drop, her boots hitting the concrete. She reached into her waistband, pulling the suppressed pistol, her eyes turning back into cold, analytical diamonds.

I adjusted my grip on my hunting knife, the blade catching the dim amber light.

"Let's go kill a Headmaster," she said.
