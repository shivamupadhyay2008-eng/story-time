Interlude: The Final Entry

DATE: October 18th. Fourteen years ago.
AUTHOR: Grand Architect [REDACTED]
STATUS: Recovered from crash site.

They think they are so clever. They think I don't see the pieces moving on the board.

For six months, I have been engineering the collapse of the European trade block. It was a masterpiece of manipulation. But someone inside the Crescent got greedy. Someone didn't want the market to crash; they wanted to own it. And now, my own society is hunting me.

Arthur warned me last night. My own brother, standing in the shadows of my study, telling me that the vote had been cast. I am to be "retired." A tragic accident on the highway. I can already smell the brake fluid they tampered with on my car.

A normal mother would panic. A normal mother would pack a bag, take her husband and her child, and try to run. But I am not normal. I am an Architect. If I run, they will hunt Bruce for the rest of his life to erase my bloodline entirely.

So, I will not run. My husband and I will get into that car tonight. We will drive into the storm, and we will die.

I have made a deal with Arthur. He is the only one I trust to execute this plan. He will take Bruce in. He will play the part of the grieving, distant uncle. He will abandon my son at a boarding school designed to forge monsters.

I know what it will do to my little boy. It will break him. It will strip away his empathy, his warmth, and his innocence. But it is the only way he survives. The world is run by wolves, and I refuse to let my son be a sheep.

Arthur will mold him. He will teach him to wear a mask. And when the time is right, Arthur will hand my son this journal.

To the men who voted for my death: you think you have won by cutting my brakes. You think the game is over.

But I do not just plan for survival. I plan for vengeance.

And my vengeance's name is Bruce.
