The Midnight Vow
Chapter One

The Intro

“We create our own demons,” a legend once said. What he forgot to add is that we spend our entire lives trying to hide them. But it doesn't really matter. The darkness always knows exactly where we are.

I have spent my whole life searching for the missing pieces to a puzzle called peace. But life, it seems, has no intention of letting me finish it.

"You still with us, Bruce?"

I blinked, letting the heavy thoughts sink back down into the depths.

"Yeah. I was just thinking about what to eat tonight."

I am a terrible liar, but people see exactly what they want to see. Their ignorance is what keeps me safe.

Sitting beside me is Jack. Average height, average looks, and constantly tagging along like a lost shadow. I despise it, but my mask doesn't let that show.

Then there is Lily. Soft-spoken, gentle, and staring at me with that quiet adoration. She thinks she has a crush on me. If she knew the actual me, she would be terrified.

I’m Bruce. At least, that's the name they know. I have spent my entire existence crafting these masks so perfectly that no one can ever reach the truth underneath. Let's just say I play the part of the handsome, charming friend far better than the morons around me play themselves.

"So, you're joining us for football, right?" Jack asked, his voice entirely too cheerful.

"Of course," I said, flashing him an effortless, practiced smile. "Give me a reason to say no."

As they smiled back, the real me watched from behind the glass. Is this really what I am supposed to be doing? Playing their games? Pretending I belong here? The question isn't whether I can handle them. The real question is... when the mask finally slips, will they be able to handle ME?
