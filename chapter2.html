The Midnight Vow
Chapter Two

Fellow Mates

My real name isn't Bruce. At least, some fractured part of my memory tells me it isn't. But I stopped caring about that a long time ago. A fake name is just another convenient layer added to the mask.

My parents died when I was three. The official story was a tragic car accident, but I’ve never believed a single word of it. My uncle took me in afterward, but no one wants a broken burden forever. By the time I was six, I was shipped off to this boarding school. I suppose being abandoned by everyone you are supposed to trust explains a lot about the way I am today.

"Read page twenty-five, line thirty... Bruce."

The teacher's sharp voice snapped me out of my thoughts. I stood up slowly, the textbook already open in my hand, and let my voice drop into a smooth, empty cadence.

"His hands were slick with blood, a profound disappointment echoing in his eyes. He feared what he had done, knowing there was no going back. As horror twisted his face, he finally realized... this was his destiny."

The classroom fell dead silent. The teacher stared at me for a long, uncomfortable moment before finally clearing his throat.

"Sit down, Bruce. You expressed those emotions... a little too well."

I took my seat, letting my eyes casually sweep over the room. My class is full of people who disgust me. Some act sickeningly overfriendly, only to tear each other apart the second backs are turned. Others are quiet, plotting their own pathetic little visions. Some hide their constant irritation behind polite smiles, and the so-called 'confident' ones still cry themselves to sleep in the dark.

I can see right through every single one of them. But as I sat there, analyzing their fragile lives, a hollow question echoed in my mind. What kind of person am I?

I waited for an answer from the darkness inside me. But for once, it was completely silent.
