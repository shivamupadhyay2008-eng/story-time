The Midnight Vow
Chapter Five

The Golden Boy

The movies make it look so easy. A few keystrokes on a stolen laptop, a whispered lie in the dark, and suddenly the villain's secrets fall right into your hands.

I ran those scenarios through my head and discarded them almost immediately. Reality is far less forgiving. The Headmaster didn't build his private empire by leaving a careless digital footprint, and the staff here are far too terrified of him to be manipulated by a student. Sneaking around would only get me caught.

If you want to infiltrate a fortress, you don't scale the walls in the dark. You make the king open the front gates and invite you inside.

Over the next few weeks, I didn't just wear my mask—I weaponized it. I became exactly what this twisted institution valued most.

I dominated the midterm exams without breaking a sweat. I scored the winning points on the football field, flashing a humble, breathless smile while Jack and the others cheered. In art class, my canvas was a calculated masterpiece—just enough emotion to show depth, but restrained enough to keep them comfortable. In every hallway, every classroom, and every conversation, I was polite, brilliant, and utterly flawless.

I didn't have a specific target yet. The photograph of my parents was a puzzle missing half its pieces, a chaotic knot I couldn't untangle. But my gut told me that proximity was power. I was setting the board, preparing myself for the moment the truth finally bled out.

It took exactly three weeks and four days for the bait to be taken.

"The Headmaster wishes to see you in his office, Bruce," my history teacher announced one afternoon, a strange hint of reverence in his voice.

I walked up to that heavy, brass-handled door in broad daylight this time. The Headmaster was waiting behind his massive mahogany desk, looking just as imposing and composed as he did in the shadows. But as he looked up at me, I saw it in his eyes.

He has no idea. He’s staring at a masterpiece of deception eighteen years in the making, and all he sees is a promising young man. He is entirely blind to the monster sitting across from him.

"Bruce," the Headmaster said smoothly, steepling his fingers. "Your recent performance across all disciplines has been nothing short of extraordinary. I believe it's time we discussed your... true potential."

I smiled back, warm, humble, and completely hollow.

Let the games begin.
