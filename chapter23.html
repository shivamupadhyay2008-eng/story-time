The Midnight Vow
Chapter Twenty-Three

The Engine of Ruin

To kill a king, you don’t fight his army. You burn his castle, slaughter his guards, and wait for him at the only door left open.

The tunnel widened, spilling out into a massive, cavernous subterranean depot. The air was thick with the smell of diesel exhaust and ancient dust. Idling on the tracks was a heavily armored, matte-black locomotive—the Headmaster’s private lifeline to the outside world.

Standing on the concrete platform, clutching a reinforced steel briefcase, was Headmaster Vance. Julian’s father. The architect of the initiation.

He was sweating, his pristine bespoke suit ruined by the damp air of the tunnels. Flanking him were two elite Praetorians, heavily armored and scanning the shadows with thermal rifles.

Lily and I didn't hide. We stepped out of the darkness and into the dim, flickering halogen light of the platform. We moved together, side by side, soaked in the blood of his security forces, walking with the terrifying, unhurried grace of apex predators who had already won.

"Kill them!" Vance shrieked, his voice cracking, losing every ounce of his aristocratic composure. "Don't let them near the train!"

The Praetorians raised their rifles, but they were too slow.

I didn't break stride. I whipped my arm forward, sending my hunting knife spinning through the air. The heavy steel blade buried itself to the hilt in the throat of the right Praetorian. He dropped his rifle, his hands flying to his neck in a desperate, gurgling panic.

Simultaneously, Lily dropped to one knee, a sleek, lethal silhouette in the dim light. Her suppressed pistol coughed twice. Pfft. Pfft. The first bullet shattered the left Praetorian’s kneecap, dropping him to the concrete. The second took him perfectly between the eyes before he even hit the ground.

In three seconds, Vance’s invincible wall was gone.

He scrambled backward, dropping his steel briefcase, his eyes wide with a pathetic, paralyzing terror. He backed up until his spine hit the cold, riveted steel of the idling train.

I walked up to him, ripping my knife free from the dead guard’s throat. The blood dripped onto the concrete, a slow, rhythmic ticking clock.

"Bruce, wait!" Vance begged, holding his trembling hands up. "You don't understand the board! Arthur—your uncle—he designed this! I was just following the Council’s orders! If you kill me, they will hunt you to the ends of the earth!"

I stepped into his space, the heat radiating off the massive diesel engine behind him. I didn't say a word. I just looked at him with the dead, empty eyes of the weapon his society had spent ten years trying to forge.

Lily stepped up beside me. She didn't look at Vance. She looked at me, a dark, wicked smile playing on her lips.

"He talks too much," she whispered.

She raised the pistol, pressing the hot suppressor directly against the center of Vance’s forehead, and pulled the trigger.

The Headmaster slumped to the concrete, dead before he realized the game was over.

The cavern fell completely silent, save for the deep, bone-rattling vibration of the locomotive engine. We had done it. We had broken the school. We had slaughtered the pack. We had severed the head of the snake.

I dropped my knife. The adrenaline of the night didn't fade; it crested, transforming into a violent, blood-drunk euphoria.

I turned to Lily. She was staring at the Headmaster’s body, her chest heaving, the pistol hanging loosely in her grip. I closed the space between us in a single step, grabbing the lapels of her torn blouse, and slammed her backward against the armored steel of the train.

She gasped, dropping the gun. It clattered to the concrete, forgotten.

The massive diesel engine idled violently beneath the metal, sending a deep, heavy vibration through the steel wall and straight into our bodies. I pinned her there, my body pressing flush against hers, crushing her back against the vibrating metal. The heat radiating off the engine was suffocating, wrapping around us like a fever.

"We won," she breathed, her dark eyes completely dilated, feral and starving as she looked up at me.

"We took the board," I rasped, my hands sliding down to grip the back of her thighs. I hoisted her up, pinning her against the vibrating steel. She wrapped her legs tightly around my waist, anchoring herself to me, her fingers tangling violently into my hair.

The friction of her body against mine, fueled by the heavy, mechanical shudder of the train behind her, was maddening. Every ragged breath we took was shared. She tasted like adrenaline and absolute power. I kissed her with a bruising, desperate force, my teeth grazing her lower lip, my hands gripping her hips hard enough to leave bruises.

She let out a sharp, hot moan against my mouth, her nails digging into the muscles of my back, pulling me impossibly closer until there was no space left between us, only heat and friction and the heavy weight of survival. We were two monsters dancing on the grave of the man who tried to cage us.

I dragged my lips down her jaw, burying my face in the crook of her neck, feeling the frantic, hammering pulse of her heart against my chest.

"You're mine," I growled against her hot skin, the vibration of the train humming through us both. "Every breath. Every shadow."

"Yours," she gasped, her hands gripping my shoulders, arching her back against the steel to press herself deeper into my grip. "Take everything, Bruce."

For a long, feverish moment, we existed only in the heat of that dark tunnel, consuming the victory, consuming each other.

Suddenly, a hydraulic hiss echoed behind her.

The heavy steel doors of the armored locomotive slid open. The interior was lit by a soft, luxurious amber glow. A digitized voice echoed from the automated intercom.

“Phoenix Protocol engaged. Destination locked: The High Council Citadel. Departure in two minutes.”

I slowly lowered her feet to the concrete, our chests still heaving, our eyes locked in the dim light. The feral heat of the moment hardened into a cold, terrifying realization of what the train represented.

Lily looked at the open doors, then back at me, her lips swollen, her eyes shining with a lethal, intoxicating brilliance.

"The Headmaster was trying to escape to the Citadel," she whispered, a dangerous smile spreading across her face.

I picked up my blood-soaked hunting knife from the concrete and grabbed her hand, our fingers interlacing perfectly.

"He was escaping," I said, stepping into the luxurious, empty train car and hitting the door seal. "We’re invading."
