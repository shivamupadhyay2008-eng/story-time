The Midnight Vow
Chapter Sixteen

First Blood

It is one thing to destroy a person on paper. It is an entirely different thing to feel their pulse stop under your hands.

The Headmaster’s voice faded, leaving a heavy, suffocating silence in its wake. The digital deadbolt on Lily’s bedroom door glowed a harsh, vibrant green.

Click.

The door unlocked. They weren't keeping us in our rooms. They were opening the cages.

I looked at Lily. She wasn't panicking. Her dark eyes were completely dilated, mapping out the architecture of the Honors Wing in her head. She walked over to her closet, pushed aside a row of pristine cashmere sweaters, and popped a false panel in the wall.

She pulled out two heavy, matte-black hunting knives.

"Chief Justice's daughter, remember?" she whispered, tossing one to me. I caught it by the leather grip. It was perfectly balanced, heavy enough to shatter bone, sharp enough to cut through lies.

"Forty of us in the building. Thirty-five have to die," I said, my voice eerily calm as I gripped the blade. "Which means right now, in the corridors outside, thirty-eight sociopaths are doing the exact same math. They are going to form packs. They will hunt the weakest first."

"To everyone else in this building, I am the soft, quiet girl who works in administration," Lily said, stepping up beside me. She tied her dark hair back into a tight, merciless knot. "They are going to come for my room first because they think I am an easy kill."

"Let them," I whispered.

We positioned ourselves on either side of the heavy wooden door. The emergency red lighting bled into the room from the corridor. For ten agonizing seconds, the only sound was the violent drumming of the rain against the steel shutters trapping us inside.

Then, we heard it.

Footsteps. Heavy, hurried, and trying to be quiet. They stopped right outside Lily’s door.

I held my breath, my muscles coiling like a spring. I could smell the ozone, the blood on my stitched palm, and the faint scent of vanilla from Lily beside me. We didn't even need to speak. We moved in absolute, terrifying synchronization.

The door violently kicked open, splintering off its hinges.

A shadow lunged into the room. It was Preston—the arrogant son of a tech billionaire, gripping a heavy steel fire extinguisher like a battering ram. He was swinging wildly, aiming for the bed where he thought Lily would be cowering.

He never even saw us.

Lily stepped out from the blind spot, slamming the pommel of her knife directly into the back of Preston’s knee. He collapsed with a choked gasp, the fire extinguisher clattering across the floorboards.

Before he could scream, I grabbed a fistful of his hair, jerking his head back and pressing the freezing edge of my hunting knife directly against his carotid artery.

Preston froze. His eyes went wide with pure, unadulterated terror as he stared up at me. He expected to find a terrified girl. Instead, he found a monster staring back at him with dead, hollow eyes.

"Bruce?" Preston choked out, his voice trembling uncontrollably. "Please... please man, they're killing people out there. Julian formed a hunting party. They're clearing the first floor. I was just looking for a place to hide—"

"You kicked the door off its hinges with a steel bludgeon, Preston," I whispered, pressing the blade just a millimeter deeper. A thin bead of crimson rolled down his neck. "You didn't come here to hide. You came here for an easy kill to secure your spot in the five."

"No! I swear—"

I looked up at Lily. Her eyes were completely cold, assessing the threat. She gave me a single, infinitesimal nod.

No mercy.

I didn't hesitate. I didn't close my eyes. I dragged the blade across his throat in one smooth, surgical motion.

The silence that followed was deafening. I slowly lowered Preston’s body to the floor, my hands coated in something warm and permanent. I had orchestrated ruins. I had shattered egos. But this was the first time I had crossed the ultimate line.

I looked down at my blood-soaked hands. I expected to feel guilt. I expected to feel sick.

But as the Grand Architect's blood pumped through my veins, the only thing I felt... was awake.

Lily stepped over the body, reaching out and gently taking my face in her clean hands. She forced me to look up, her thumbs resting softly against my cheekbones.

"Are you with me?" she whispered, her eyes searching mine, anchoring me to the violence.

"I'm with you," I breathed, the absolute truth ringing in my chest.

"Good," Lily said, a dark, beautiful smile curving her lips. She let go of my face and picked up the heavy steel fire extinguisher from the floor. "Because Julian Vance thinks he owns the first floor. And I think it’s time we showed him who actually runs this school."

We stepped out of the bedroom and into the blood-red corridor. The hunt had officially begun.
