The Midnight Vow
Chapter Eleven

The Trojan Horse

A normal person breaks when they realize their entire life is a lie. But I have never been normal. When the lie shattered, I didn't feel grief. I felt a violent, suffocating rage that demanded to be let out.

I stood in the dim, gaslit archive, staring into the cold eyes of my uncle Arthur. The heavy leather journal felt like a block of ice in my hands.

"It wasn't a rival syndicate that killed your mother, Bruce," Arthur said, his voice echoing off the damp stone walls. "And it wasn't the police. The order came from inside the Crescent's High Council."

He took a slow step toward me. "But they didn't cut the brake lines on her car. They didn't have to."

I looked from Arthur to the Headmaster, who stood silently in the shadows, his hands clasped behind his back.

"I cut the brakes," Arthur confessed, his expression entirely devoid of remorse.

For a fraction of a second, the world went completely silent.

The mask I had spent twelve years perfecting didn't just slip. It shattered. I didn't think. I just moved.

Before Arthur could take another breath, I crossed the distance between us, my forearm slamming into his throat, pinning him violently against the stone wall. The heavy journal hit the floor with a thud. My fingers dug into his collar, my pulse roaring in my ears.

"Give me one reason," I whispered, my voice trembling with a pure, murderous hatred I didn't know I was capable of. "Give me one reason why I shouldn't crush your windpipe right now."

The Headmaster tensed, reaching into his jacket, but Arthur held up a hand, gasping for air. He didn't fight back. He just stared into my eyes, completely unafraid.

"Because she begged me to do it," Arthur choked out.

I froze, the pressure on his throat loosening just a fraction of an inch.

"Your mother didn't just manipulate markets, Bruce," Arthur rasped, catching his breath. "She discovered what the High Council was actually planning. A manufactured global collapse. The starvation of millions to force a restructuring of the world order. She found the digital kill-switch for their operation, and she encrypted it."

Arthur reached up, gently gripping my wrist. "The Council found out. They dispatched a team of 'Interrogators' to your house. Do you know what Crescent Interrogators do, Bruce? They were going to keep your parents alive for weeks. They were going to skin you alive in front of her until she gave them the decryption keys."

My grip faltered. The horrific, suffocating logic began to bleed through my rage.

"Your mother called me ten minutes before the Interrogators arrived," Arthur said softly, his own eyes finally showing a flicker of ancient grief. "She told me to cut the brake lines and force a catastrophic failure on the highway. It was the only way to make the Council believe the kill-switch burned with her. It was the only way you would survive."

I slowly let go of his throat, stepping back. My chest heaved as I stared at my hands.

They didn't abandon me. They sacrificed themselves to save me. And they died knowing I would be left completely alone in the dark.

The grief inside me didn't fade, but it changed. Under the immense pressure of the truth, the sorrow compressed into something hard, sharp, and utterly lethal.

Arthur adjusted his collar, watching the transformation happen in my eyes. "If I had raised you with love, you would be a target. I abandoned you to this school to forge you into a weapon. Because the Council still runs the world. And your mother’s final order to me was to make sure her son was ready to burn them to the ground."

Arthur gestured toward the ceiling of the archive, pointing toward the dormitories hundreds of feet above us.

"The Council members who voted for your mother's death... their children sleep in those beds," the Headmaster finally spoke, stepping into the light. "Senator Vance. CEO Harrison. Chief Justice Sterling. They send their heirs here to be groomed."

Arthur stepped forward, picking up the journal and placing a heavy, silver ring on top of it. It bore the crest of the Crescent Moon pierced by a dagger.

"Julian Vance was just your entrance exam, Bruce," Arthur whispered. "You proved you can break a golden boy. Now, I want you to break all of them. Systematically dismantle the heirs of the High Council from the inside out. Blackmail them. Corrupt them. And when their parents finally look to the next generation to inherit their empires, they will find that you already own them all."

I am not a victim of a tragic backstory. I am the Trojan Horse.

I looked at Arthur. I understood his logic, but I would never forgive him for it. I would use him. I would play his game. But when the Council was in ashes, my uncle would pay for his sins, too.

I reached out and slid the heavy silver ring onto my right hand. It fit perfectly.

"Tell me who's next," I said, my voice cold, dead, and ready for war.
