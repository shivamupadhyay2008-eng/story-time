The Midnight Vow
Chapter Seven

The Weak Link

Flawless people do not exist. Perfection is just a well-funded illusion. If a structure looks unbreakable from the outside, it simply means the rot is hidden deep in the foundation.

It was Tuesday afternoon, three days before my midnight deadline. Julian Vance was sitting across the courtyard, holding court at a table of admiring students. He looked like a future senator—perfect hair, a relaxed, confident smile, and an effortless charm.

I watched him for twenty minutes. Most people look at Julian and see a king. I looked at him and saw a man carrying a very heavy, invisible weight. He checked his watch four times. His left leg was bouncing under the table. His smile never quite reached his eyes.

Julian was stressed. And stressed people make mistakes.

"He's intimidating, isn't he?"

I turned. Lily was standing next to me, holding a stack of library books. She followed my gaze toward Julian.

"I suppose so," I said, offering her my gentlest, most unassuming smile. "He just always seems to have everything perfectly under control."

"Not always," Lily murmured, adjusting her books. "I work in the student administration office on Thursdays. Last week, I saw Julian arguing with Mr. Harrison, the finance director. Julian looked... frantic. But the second I walked in, they both smiled and pretended they were just talking about the debate team budget."

God, I love how observant quiet people are. They see everything because no one ever looks at them.

"That's strange," I said softly, pretending to be mildly concerned. "I hope he's not in any trouble."

"I'm sure it's nothing," Lily smiled, entirely unaware that she had just handed me the keys to Julian's destruction.

Mr. Harrison. The finance director. A man known for a severe gambling addiction that the school quietly swept under the rug two years ago. If Julian was frantic about money, the golden boy wasn't just arrogant. He was in debt.

I didn't need to break into Julian's room in the dark. I just needed him to leave his bag unattended for exactly sixty seconds.

At lunch the next day, I sat next to Jack. Julian’s table was exactly fifteen feet away. Julian’s leather messenger bag sat on the floor beside his chair.

"Hey Jack," I said casually, pointing to a girl across the cafeteria. "Isn't that Sarah? I think she’s been staring at you."

Jack’s head snapped up. His face flushed with instant excitement. "Wait, seriously? Should I go talk to her?"

"You miss 100% of the shots you don't take," I smiled encouragingly.

Jack stood up, nervously smoothing his shirt, and grabbed his tray. He was so focused on Sarah that he wasn't looking at his feet. As he walked past Julian's table, I subtly hooked my foot around the leg of Jack's chair, pushing it just an inch into his path.

Jack tripped. His tray flew out of his hands, sending a tidal wave of hot coffee and soup directly onto Julian’s crisp white shirt.

The cafeteria erupted into gasps. Julian leaped up, his perfect mask slipping for a fraction of a second to reveal pure, venomous rage, before he forced it back down.

"I am so, so sorry!" Jack stammered, horrified.

"It's... fine," Julian said through gritted teeth, dabbing uselessly at his ruined shirt. "I'll just go to the locker room and change."

Julian stormed out of the cafeteria. He was so distracted by the public humiliation that he left his leather bag sitting on the floor.

The cafeteria was staring at Jack. Everyone's eyes were on the mess. No one was looking at me.

I casually bent down to tie my shoe. In one fluid motion, I unlatched Julian's bag, slipped my hand inside, and pulled out his private leather-bound planner. I slid it under my jacket, tied my shoe, and sat back up, continuing to eat my sandwich as if nothing had happened.

Total elapsed time: six seconds.

The Headmaster wants Julian leashed. Now, let's see exactly what the golden boy is trying so desperately to hide.
