The Midnight Vow
Chapter Nine

The Strings

The old greenhouse smelled like damp earth and rot. The glass ceiling was cracked, letting in jagged slices of moonlight that cut through the overgrown vines. It was the perfect place to bury something. Or someone.

I stepped through the rusted iron doors precisely at midnight.

Julian was already there, pacing like a caged animal. When he heard my footsteps, he spun around, his hands clenched into fists. But the moment the moonlight hit my face, his entire body froze. The absolute, unadulterated shock in his eyes was almost intoxicating.

"Bruce?" he breathed, his voice cracking. "What the hell are you doing here?"

He was trying to process it. His brain was desperately trying to reconcile the polite, harmless kid from his calculus class with the predator who had cornered him.

The shock lasted exactly three seconds before his survival instincts kicked in. Julian’s spine straightened. The arrogant, untouchable golden boy returned, masking his panic with rage. He reached into his designer jacket and threw a thick, white envelope onto a nearby potting table. It landed with a heavy thud.

"You?" Julian scoffed, taking a step toward me, trying to use his height to intimidate me. "You think you can play this game with me? There's ten grand in there. Take it, keep your mouth shut, and walk away. If you ever look at me again, I will have you expelled so fast your head will spin. We're done here."

He thought he was a wolf. He thought he could just buy his way out of the slaughterhouse.

I didn't look at the money. I didn't take a step back. I just smiled—not my usual, warm, practiced smile, but something cold, dead, and entirely real.

"October 14th," I said quietly.

Julian stopped dead in his tracks.

"Two a.m. Route 9," I continued, my voice smooth and surgical. "You were drunk. You hit her, and you drove away. William Harrison has been bleeding your debate team endowment dry to pay off the traffic cameras. Your encrypted ledger was pathetic, by the way. It took me less than forty minutes to break."

Julian’s face drained of all color. His jaw trembled, but he forced a desperate laugh. "You have no proof! It's your word against mine, you psycho!"

"I have the original ledger," I whispered, stepping into his space. "And I have a pre-scheduled script running on an encrypted server. If my heart stops, or if I am not back in my room by 1:00 A.M. to enter a passcode, every decoded page, every bank transfer, and the exact coordinates of the hit-and-run get emailed to the local police, the school board, and your father’s biggest political rivals."

I tilted my head, watching the last shred of his ego disintegrate. "Are you really in a position to threaten me, Julian?"

His knees actually gave out. He slumped back against the wooden table, the envelope of cash falling to the dirt floor. The flawless king of the school shattered into a terrified, hyperventilating child.

"Please," he choked out, tears finally spilling over his cheeks. "Please, Bruce, my dad will kill me. I'll do whatever you want. I'll give you whatever you want. Just tell me what to do."

There it is. Absolute submission.

"The Headmaster sent me," I said coldly. "He wanted me to put you on a leash for the Crescent. He wanted you to belong to the society."

Julian looked up, his eyes wide with fresh horror.

"But the Headmaster doesn't own you," I said, leaning in so close he could hear my breathing. "I do. You are going to go back to him, and you are going to play the part of a broken, obedient servant to the Crescent. But your true loyalty belongs to me. I am your God now, Julian. Do you understand?"

Julian swallowed hard, completely broken. "I understand."

I finally have my inside man. The Crescent invited me to sit at their table, but they have no idea I’m already holding all the cards.
