The Midnight Vow
Chapter Twelve

The Spider's Web

The greatest trick a predator can pull isn't hiding in the shadows. It is standing directly in the light, convincing you that it is prey.

The heavy silver ring felt cold on my right hand. I didn't take my eyes off Arthur as he walked across the underground archive and unlocked a dark iron filing cabinet.

"The High Council has five seats," Arthur said, pulling out a thin, black dossier. "We start by blinding them. We take out their eyes, their ears, and their heirs. We start with the man who cast the deciding vote to send the Interrogators to your mother."

He threw the file onto the pedestal between us.

"Chief Justice Sterling," Arthur stated flatly. "He is paranoid, brilliant, and untouchable. But like all of them, he sent his bloodline here to be initiated. His daughter is his greatest weakness, and she is your first official target."

I opened the black folder. Staring back at me was a crisp, high-resolution surveillance photo.

My heart completely stopped.

The girl in the photo had soft eyes, gentle features, and an oversized cardigan.

It was Lily.

"Lilian Sterling," Arthur said, completely oblivious to the shock paralyzing my lungs. "She is highly intelligent and completely ruthless. She operates deep undercover. We believe her father tasked her with monitoring the student body for threats."

Lily.

My mind violently rewound through the last three years. The soft touches on my arm. The quiet adoration in her eyes. The innocent little conversations.

“I work in the student administration office on Thursdays. Last week, I saw Julian arguing with Mr. Harrison...”

A cold sweat broke out across my neck as the horrifying truth locked into place. Lily hadn't just 'accidentally' noticed Julian’s panic. She hadn't innocently handed me the key to his destruction. She had weaponized me. She saw Julian as a threat, and she manipulated me into pulling the trigger to eliminate him.

I thought I was the mastermind playing a school full of sheep. I didn't realize I had been sitting next to an apex predator every single day at lunch.

"She doesn't know who you really are," Arthur continued. "To her, you are just a useful idiot. You need to get close to her, Bruce. Find her leverage. And break her."

I closed the folder, forcing the ice back into my veins. "Consider it done."

The next morning, the library was mostly empty. The morning sun poured through the stained-glass windows, illuminating the dust motes dancing in the air.

Lily was sitting at a corner table, reading a heavy textbook. She looked like a painting of perfect, fragile innocence.

I walked over and sat across from her. I didn't smile. I didn't put on the charming, warm mask she was used to. I just stared at her.

She looked up, a soft, welcoming smile spreading across her lips. "Morning, Bruce. You look intense today. Did you sleep okay?"

"Julian Vance stepped down from the debate team," I said quietly, keeping my voice dead flat. "He withdrew his Ivy League applications. His entire life collapsed overnight."

Lily blinked, her eyes widening in perfect, manufactured sympathy. "Oh my god. That's terrible. I wonder what happened?"

"I think you know exactly what happened, Lilian."

The air in the library seemed to instantly freeze.

Lily stopped moving. Her breathing slowed. The warm, innocent light in her eyes completely vanished, replaced by an abyss so dark and calculating it mirrored my own.

Slowly, deliberately, she closed her textbook. She leaned forward, resting her chin on her perfectly manicured hands. A terrifying, razor-sharp smile carved its way across her face.

"Well," Lily whispered, her voice dropping an octave into a smooth, venomous purr. "I was wondering how long it would take you to finally wake up."

She let her eyes drop to my right hand, lingering on the silver ring I had just earned.

"Nice ring, Bruce," she smiled softly. "Or... should I call you by your real name?"
